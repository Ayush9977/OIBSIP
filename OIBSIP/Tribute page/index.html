General Bipin Rawat

General Bipin Rawat PVSM UYSM AVSM YSM SM VSM ADC (16 March 1958 - 8 December 2021) was an Indian military officer who was a four-star general of the Indian Army.[5] He served as the first Chief of Defence Staff (CDS) of the Indian Armed Forces from January 2020 until his death in a helicopter crash in December 2021. Prior to taking over as the CDS, he served as the 57th Chairman of the Chiefs of Staff Committee (Chairman COSC) of the Indian Armed Forces as well as 26th Chief of the Army Staff (COAS) of the Indian Army.

Early life and education

  • 1) Bipin Rawat was born on March 16, 1958, in Pauri town of Pauri Garhwal district. He comes from a Hindu Garhwali Rajput family with a long tradition of serving in the Indian Army.
  • 2) His father, Lakshman Singh Rawat, was commissioned into the 11 Gorkha Rifles in 1951 and retired as Deputy Chief of the Army Staff in 1988 as a Lieutenant General.
  • 3) Rawat completed his education at Cambrian Hall school in Dehradun and St. Edward's School in Shimla.
  • 4) Rawat graduated first in the order of merit from the Indian Military Academy and was awarded the 'Sword of Honour'.

Military career

  • 1) General Bipin Rawat served in the Indian Army for over 40 years before retiring in 2019.
  • 2) He was commissioned into the 5th Battalion of the 11 Gorkha Rifles in December 1978.
  • 3) He has held various command, staff, and instructional positions in his career.
  • 4) He has held various command, staff, and instructional positions in his career.
  • 5) General Rawat was appointed as the Chief of the Army Staff (COAS) in December 2016, succeeding General Dalbir Singh Suhag.
  • 6) During his tenure as the COAS, he initiated several reforms in the Indian Army, including restructuring of the officer cadre and modernization of the armed forces.
  • 7) He retired from his position as COAS in December 2019 and was appointed as India's first Chief of Defence Staff (CDS) in January 2020.

Death and legacy

  • 1) On 8 December 2021, Rawat, his wife and members of his staff were amongst 10 passengers and 4 crew members aboard an Indian Air Force Mil Mi-17 helicopter flight en route from the Sulur Air Force Base to the Defence Services Staff College (DSSC), Wellington, where Rawat was to deliver a lecture.
  • 2) Rawat was 63 at the time of his death.
  • 3) Rawat and his wife were cremated according to Hindu rituals with full military honours and 17 gun salute at Brar Square Crematorium in Delhi Cantonment on 10 December 2021.
  • 4) On Rawat's 65th birth anniversary, the Indian Navy instituted the General Bipin Rawat rolling trophy for the Woman Agniveer Trainee standing First in Overall Order of Merit
